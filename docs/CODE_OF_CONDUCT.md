# Code of Conduct

*This is part of an upfront ideology doc suite, written before the code. See [Status](README.md#status) in the main README.*

NOCC is built by people who think you shouldn't need permission to have a private conversation. Act accordingly.

## The rule

Don't be a jerk. That covers most of it:

- No harassment, no hate speech, no targeting people over who they are.
- Disagree about code all you want. Attack the argument, not the person.
- This is a tool for a political fight against mass surveillance, not a customer support product. We're not here to hand-hold, but we're also not here to be cruel to people who show up in good faith, **including beginners**.
- Keep it on-topic. Issues and PRs are for the project, not a soapbox for unrelated causes.

## Enforcement

Violate this and you get warned once. Keep it up and you're blocked/banned from the repo, no drawn-out process, no committee. Maintainers' judgment call.

## Reporting

See something bad? Open a GitHub issue if it's minor and public-safe, or email the maintainers directly (see repo contact info) if it involves harassment or anything you'd rather not post publicly.

That's the whole policy. We'd rather spend our time shipping encryption than writing conduct paperwork.
